The Problem: Street Children in Kampala

Understanding the scale and severity of the issue

A Growing Crisis

In Kampala, Uganda's capital city, an estimated 15,000 children live and work on the streets. These children face daily threats to their safety, health, and future prospects. Without intervention, they remain trapped in a cycle of poverty and vulnerability.

Street children are among the most marginalized and vulnerable populations in Uganda. They lack access to basic necessities like food, shelter, healthcare, and education, making them susceptible to exploitation, abuse, and long-term psychological trauma.

Daily Challenges

  • Lack of Shelter: Sleeping on streets, in markets, or in abandoned buildings exposes children to violence, harsh weather, and health risks.
  • Food Insecurity: Many street children survive on one meal a day, often from garbage or begging, leading to malnutrition.
  • No Access to Education: Without schooling, children miss critical development opportunities and remain trapped in poverty.
  • Health Risks: Limited access to healthcare increases vulnerability to diseases, injuries, and mental health issues.
  • Exploitation: Street children are vulnerable to child labor, trafficking, and various forms of abuse.

Root Causes

Understanding why children end up on the streets

Poverty

Extreme poverty forces families to send children to work on the streets or leads to family breakdowns where children are abandoned.

Family Breakdown

Death of parents, divorce, domestic violence, and neglect leave children with no support system and push them to the streets.

Lack of Education

When families cannot afford school fees, children often end up on streets instead of in classrooms, limiting their future opportunities.

Peer Influence

Children already on streets often encourage others to join them, creating a cycle that's difficult to break.

Health Issues

Families struggling with HIV/AIDS, disabilities, or chronic illnesses often cannot care for children, leading to abandonment.

Rural-Urban Migration

Children migrating to cities in search of opportunities often end up on streets when expected support fails to materialize.

Our Holistic Approach

How Courage Charity addresses the problem

A Multi-Faceted Solution

At Courage Charity, we understand that addressing street children's needs requires a comprehensive approach that tackles both immediate needs and underlying causes.

  • Outreach & Identification: Our team regularly visits known street child hotspots to build trust and identify children needing help.
  • Immediate Rescue: We provide emergency shelter, food, medical care, and psychological first aid to newly rescued children.
  • Rehabilitation: Children receive counseling, education assessment, and life skills training in a safe environment.
  • Family Reunification: Where possible and safe, we work to reunite children with their families, providing support to prevent re-streeting.
  • Education & Skills: We enroll children in schools or vocational training programs to equip them for self-sufficient futures.
  • Community Integration: We work with local communities to reduce stigma and create supportive environments for reintegrated children.
  • Advocacy: We advocate for policy changes and increased resources for street children at local and national levels.

Why Our Approach Works

Our model has proven successful because we address the whole child—their physical, emotional, educational, and social needs. We don't just remove children from streets; we provide pathways to sustainable, dignified lives.
